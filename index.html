Full Day Schedule

What You Will
Actually Do

Not slides. Not lectures. You sit, you build, you understand — 7 hours of live project work.

Morning · 9:00 – 11:30 AM

Build the Architecture

Start from a real CAD drawing. Link it into Revit, trace walls over it, place doors, windows, floors and ceilings. Understand how a building model actually comes together.

Midday · 11:30 AM – 1:30 PM

Route the HVAC System

Place air terminals at the correct heights. Draw ducts connecting them. Understand why positioning matters and how mechanical engineers think inside a building.

Afternoon · 2:00 – 3:30 PM

Add Electrical Systems

Place lighting fixtures, switches, and route conduits through the same building. Understand how electrical planning fits around what's already there.

End of Day · 3:30 – 4:00 PM

See Everything Together

Open the 3D coordination view. Watch architecture, HVAC and electrical systems appear in the same model. Understand what coordination actually means on a real project.

FAQ

Frequently Asked
Questions

Who is this workshop for?

This workshop is for 3rd year and final year engineering students, fresh graduates, and anyone looking to move into BIM & MEP roles. No prior Revit experience is required.

Do I need prior BIM or Revit experience?

No prior experience is needed. The workshop starts from the basics and takes you through the full workflow step by step alongside an industry professional.

What will I take home after the workshop?

You'll receive a full day of hands-on workflow experience, an industry-recognised certificate, and a clear understanding of BIM careers and MEP coordination workflows.

Is this workshop only offline?

Yes. This is a fully offline, in-person workshop. The hands-on nature of BIM work requires you to be physically present to get the full experience.

How many seats are available?

Strictly limited to 30 participants. Once those seats are filled, registration closes with no exceptions. Register early to secure your spot.
